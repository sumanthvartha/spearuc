Section 01 — Current state

How our current platform actually works

Our current platform (SpearUC) is fundamentally a telephone call broadcasting system. When the admin starts a meeting, the platform literally dials every phone number using the regular telecom network (PSTN). Here's the exact mechanism:

1

Admin clicks "Start"

Admin logs into a web dashboard, selects the group, and clicks Start Meeting. Nothing special here — standard web interface.

2

Platform dials EVERY number

The system places a real telephone call to every single phone number in the list — using the PSTN (regular phone network). This is the expensive part.

3

Users pick up and listen

Users receive a normal incoming call. They pick up. They hear the admin speaking through a conference bridge. No internet or app needed.

The key thing to understand

Every participant, for every minute, for every meeting — the platform is paying a real telecom carrier Rs 0.40–0.60 per minute per call. A 20-minute meeting with 500 people = 10,000 call-minutes = Rs 4,000–6,000 in telecom charges alone. There is no internet-based alternative in the current platform. It's 100% PSTN, 100% of the time.

Section 03 — Dependencies and risks

We are locked into a single vendor with zero alternatives

Our current platform creates multiple layers of dependency that put our operations at risk.

Vendor lock-in

All our contact data, meeting history, recordings, and workflows live inside SpearUC. No export capability. No API. If they shut down or raise prices, we have zero fallback.

Stale product (no updates since 2017)

The platform website dates from 2015. The mobile app was last updated in 2017. No new features, no AI capabilities, no modern integrations. The product is effectively abandoned.

No security certifications

Zero mention of SOC 2, ISO 27001, or DPDP Act compliance. No end-to-end encryption documentation. No audit trails. For a platform handling our sensitive business communications, this is a liability.

Opaque pricing with no transparency

No published pricing. No per-meeting cost breakdown. No way to audit whether we're being overcharged. We can't tell what percentage of our bill is telecom pass-through vs. platform margin.

Section 04 — The opportunity

A hybrid model that cuts costs by 60–80%

The current platform uses expensive PSTN calls for 100% of users. But in 2026, 75% of our 500 contacts have smartphones with internet. The insight: only use paid phone calls for the 25% who actually need them.

SMARTPHONE USERS (75%)

Connected via internet — Rs 0 per call

375 users receive a push notification or SMS with a meeting link. They tap once. Their browser connects to a WebRTC audio room via their existing internet connection. Admin speaks, they hear. Exact same experience — but costs us nothing in telecom.

Cost per meeting (375 users × 20 min)
Rs 0
BASIC PHONE USERS (25%)

Phone call via PSTN — Rs 0.50/min

125 users on basic phones still receive a regular phone call, exactly like today. Same experience for them. But now we're only paying telecom charges for 25% of users instead of 100%.

Cost per meeting (125 users × 20 min)
Rs 1,250
Both channels feed into the same audio room

The admin doesn't know or care which channel each user is on. A smartphone user on WebRTC and a basic phone user on PSTN hear each other perfectly in the same conference. The system routes automatically based on each contact's phone type.

Section 06 — Beyond cost savings

What we gain that the current platform can never offer

Cost savings alone justify the switch. But the alternative platform also unlocks capabilities our current vendor hasn't built in 10 years.

Real-time analytics dashboard

See exactly who joined, when, via which channel, and for how long. Per-meeting cost tracking. Exportable reports. No more guessing whether the message reached everyone.

AI meeting transcription and summaries

Every meeting automatically transcribed in English, Hindi, Telugu, and Tamil. AI generates a 3-line summary sent to all participants via SMS after the meeting. No more "what did the admin say?"

DTMF feedback collection

After a broadcast, phone users hear: "Press 1 if understood, 2 for callback." Instant structured feedback without a live Q&A. Dashboard shows real-time response rates.

Complete data ownership

All contacts, recordings, analytics, and history in our own database. Full data export. Open API for integration with CRM and other tools. No vendor lock-in, ever.
